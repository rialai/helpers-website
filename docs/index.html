Startup Activity

A cooperative that funds demand, software, and operational trust.

Helpers.ie covers the cost of advertising to acquire clients, then designs, builds, and deploys software inside microbusinesses so that new customer demand can be directed toward the operators who cooperate responsibly and deliver consistently.

Instead of rewarding whoever shouts the loudest or spends the most on lead generation, the system prioritizes the people who actually commit to shared standards, transparent operations, and dependable execution.

How the model works

The cooperative absorbs growth costs up front, installs working software inside the business, and then routes clients toward the members who take cooperation seriously.

Client acquisition Advertising funded by the cooperative
Software rollout Design, development, and implementation inside microbusinesses
Routing priority Given to the most responsible cooperative participants

What is funded

Advertising spend for customer acquisition, plus the technical work needed to install and operationalize software where the business actually runs.

Who it is for

Microbusinesses and hands-on operators who want more clients but are willing to be measured by reliability, transparency, and cooperative discipline.

What gets prioritized

The businesses that handle shared infrastructure responsibly, respect agreed processes, and prove they can be trusted with incoming customer demand.
